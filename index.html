About Me

▶ Curriculum vitae

Hello, I am an Assistant Professor in the School of Electronics and Information Engineering at Korea Aerospace University (KAU), where I direct the Intelligent and Interactive Systems Lab (IIS Lab). My work has been funded by grants and contracts from the National Research Foundation of Korea (NRF), the Electronics and Telecommunications Research Institute (ETRI), the GRRC Program of Gyeonggi Province and Korea Aerospace University (KAU). I work in computer vision and its application to build intelligent and interactive systems (e.g., robots). I have extensive research experience about a lot of software tools like (Tensorflow, Robot Operating System (ROS), and among many other technologies for building diverse intelligent and interactive systems.

Before joining KAU, I spent about two years in the ObjectVideo Labs of Alarm.com. There I worked mostly on vessel recognition in maritime surveillance videos with Office of Naval Research (ONR) to support the US Navy’s mission.

Before joining ObjectVideo Labs, I was a graduate student in the School of Informatics, Computing, and Engineering at Indiana University. I worked with Professor David Crandall on Computer Vision for Activity Learning and Professor Selma Šabanović on Human-Robot Interaction. I completed the Ph.D degree in July 2018.

Before IU, I was a software engineer at Samsung Electronics and received B.S. in Information and Communication Engineering from Sungkyunkwan University (SKKU) in 2006 and M.S in Electrical and Computer Engineering from SKKU in 2008.
